Naan Recipe

Description

Naan bread is a soft, pillowy flatbread with a hint of chewiness and light tang from yogurt or yeast. Traditionally cooked in a tandoor, this version is made on the stove, giving it a slightly smoky char. Brushed with butter or ghee, it's perfect for scooping up curries or enjoying on its own!

Ingredients

  • Sugar 1tsp
  • Water 120ml
  • Active Dry Yeast 10g
  • Flour (at least 300g)
  • Plain Yoghurt
  • Salt 1tsp
  • Oil 1tbsp
  • Butter 1tbsp for Brushing

Steps

  1. Mix the yeast and lukewarm water in a bowl and wait until bloomin to ensure the yeast is alive.
  2. Add about 10% of the flour to create a slurry.
  3. Add the oil, yoghurt and salt. Mix thorougly.
  4. Add the remaining flour and stir with a spoon.
  5. Once all the flour is incorporated move onto a floured surface.
  6. Work the dough for roughly 10 minutes, adding more flour if too sticky.
  7. After 10 minutes you should have a slightly sticky, "glossy" doughball.
  8. Let rise for about 1 hour or until doubled in size
  9. Divide into 8 similar size balls (rougly 70g each).
  10. Flatten out by hand.
  11. Cook in an oiled, medium hot pan. Until slightly charred spots start appearing.
  12. Once all the dough balls are cooked, brush each of them with some melted butter and optionally with some garlic and serve!
